Net Metering

How Net Metering Works:

Net metering allows owners of solar energy systems to earn credits for the surplus electricity they produce and feed back into the grid. When a solar system generates more electricity than is used on-site, the excess power flows back through a green meter, which tracks both the power drawn from the grid and the power sent back. The electricity company then issues credits for this surplus energy, which can offset future electricity bills during periods when solar generation is lower, such as at night or on cloudy days. Each billing cycle calculates the net amount subtracting any credits from total grid usage, effectively lowering the overall electricity cost. This system not only benefits users financially by reducing their energy bills but also helps stabilize the energy grid by integrating more renewable power, supporting a cleaner, more sustainable energy future.

Energy Alternatives’ offers advanced net metering solutions that empower our customers to harness the full potential of their solar energy systems. 1. Installation and Connection:

Our professional team installs a green meter, which is essential for measuring both the electricity you consume from the grid and the surplus energy your solar panels send back. This meter records the flow of energy in both directions, import and export, so that any excess energy you generate can be accounted for.

2. Seamless Integration:

We ensure that the net metering system is seamlessly integrated with your solar energy setup, following all regulatory and technical requirements. This ensures that your system operates smoothly, whether you’re drawing energy from the grid or sending energy back.

3. Earning Credits:

When your solar panels generate more energy than your home or business consumes, the surplus electricity is automatically exported to the grid. Your utility company then provides you with credits for this surplus energy. These credits are applied to your future energy bills, reducing your overall cost.

4. Reduction in Energy Bills:

Net metering has a significant financial benefit for solar system owners. By exporting excess electricity, you reduce your dependence on electricity from the grid, resulting in lower monthly utility bills.

5. Offsetting Future Bills:

During periods when your energy consumption exceeds your solar generation (such as at night or during low-sun periods), you can use the credits you’ve accumulated to offset your energy usage from the grid. This means lower electricity bills in the long term.

6. Monthly and Long-Term Savings:

Net metering has a significant financial benefit for solar system owners. By exporting excess electricity, you reduce your dependence on electricity from the grid, resulting in lower monthly utility bills.

7. Payback Period:

Over time, the savings from net metering can help you recover the initial cost of installing a solar energy system more quickly. This makes solar not just an environmentally conscious choice, but also a sound financial investment.

8. Annual Roll-Over of Credits:

In many cases, any unused credits from one billing cycle can be rolled over into the next, ensuring that you continue to benefit even when solar generation is lower (e.g., in winter).

9. Contribution to the Grid:

Supporting the Energy Grid By feeding surplus energy back into the grid, you are not only reducing your own costs but also contributing to a more balanced and sustainable energy infrastructure. Your solar-generated electricity helps reduce the strain on conventional power plants, many of which rely on fossil fuels.

10. Environmental Impact:

Net metering encourages the use of renewable energy sources, which reduces carbon emissions and dependence on non-renewable energy. This helps Pakistan move toward a more sustainable energy future.
